The Role of Media in Politics

One of the primary roles of media in politics is to inform the public about current events, policies, and political issues.

Through newspapers, television, radio, and online platforms, the media provides citizens with essential information that helps them make informed decisions during elections and understand the actions of government officials.

Media outlets have the power to shape public opinion by framing political issues, presenting biased or selective information, and influencing the public discourse.

The media serves as a watchdog by holding political leaders and institutions accountable for their actions.
